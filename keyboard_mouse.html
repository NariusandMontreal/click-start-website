Using a Keyboard & Mouse

Home Row Keys and Finger Placement

On a standard QWERTY keyboard, the home row keys are usually A, S, D, F for your left hand and J, K, L, ; (semicolon) for your right hand. Your index fingers will rest on the F and J keys, which often have a small raised bump to help you find them without looking.

How to Use Shift, Enter, Backspace

  • Shift: Hold down Shift and press a letter key to type an uppercase letter. Use it with number keys to type symbols.
  • Enter (or Return): Press Enter at the end of a line to start a new line or to submit information in forms.
  • Backspace (or Delete): Press Backspace to delete the character to the left of the cursor.

Left-Click vs. Right-Click vs. Double-Click

  • Left-Click: The primary click, usually used to select items, open programs, or click links.
  • Right-Click: Often opens a context menu with more options related to what you've clicked on.
  • Double-Click: Quickly pressing the left mouse button twice in a row, usually used to open files or applications.

Scrolling and Dragging Items

  • Scrolling: Use the scroll wheel (if your mouse has one) or two fingers on a trackpad to move up or down on a page.
  • Dragging: Click and hold the left mouse button on an item, move the mouse to a new location, and then release the button to move the item.
