How to Use Email

Creating an Email Account (e.g., Gmail or Outlook)

  • Go to the website of the email provider (e.g., Gmail or Outlook).
  • Click on "Create account" or a similar button.
  • Fill out the registration form with your details.
  • Choose a username and a strong password.
  • Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the setup.
  • You might need to verify your account using a phone number.

Sending and Receiving Emails

  • To send an email: Click on "Compose" or "New Email."
  • Enter the recipient's email address in the "To" field.
  • Write a brief summary of your email in the "Subject" field.
  • Type your message in the main body of the email.
  • Click "Send."
  • To receive an email: New emails will appear in your "Inbox."
  • Click on an email in your inbox to open and read it.

How to Attach Files and Photos

  • When composing an email, look for a paperclip icon or a button that says "Attach."
  • Click on it and a file explorer window will open.
  • Select the file(s) you want to attach and click "Open" or "Attach."
  • The attached file(s) will usually appear below the subject line.

How to Open Attachments

  • When you receive an email with an attachment, you'll see an icon (like a paperclip) next to the email in your inbox or within the email itself.
  • Click on the attachment to download it to your computer.
  • Once downloaded, you can usually click on the file to open it. Make sure you trust the sender before opening attachments.

How to Avoid Spam or Suspicious Links

  • Be cautious of emails from unknown senders.
  • Don't click on links in emails that seem suspicious or ask for personal information.
  • Look out for poor grammar or spelling in emails.
  • Never share your password in response to an email.
  • Spam emails often promise unrealistic rewards or create a sense of urgency.

Email Etiquette

  • Start with a polite greeting (e.g., "Dear [Name]," or "Hello [Name],").
  • Be clear and concise in your message.
  • Avoid writing in all capital letters, as it can be seen as shouting.
  • Use proper grammar and spelling.
  • End with a polite closing (e.g., "Sincerely," "Best regards,").
  • Proofread your email before sending it.
