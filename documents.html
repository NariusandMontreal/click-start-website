Creating & Saving a Document

Creating a Document

You can create documents using word processing software like:

  • Google Docs: A free, web-based application. Go to Google Docs and click "Blank" to start a new document.
  • Microsoft Word: A popular desktop application (may require a purchase or subscription). Open the Word application on your computer and choose "Blank document."

Once you have a new document open, you can start typing your text.

Saving a File

  • Click on "File" in the menu (usually at the top left).
  • Select "Save" or "Save As..."
  • Choose a location: Select where you want to save the file (e.g., your Documents folder, a USB drive, or cloud storage like Google Drive or OneDrive).
  • Name your file: In the "File name" field, type a descriptive name for your document (e.g., "My First Document," "Computer Skills Notes").
  • Click "Save."

Naming and Organizing Files

  • Use clear and descriptive names for your files so you can easily find them later.
  • Organize your files into folders (e.g., "School Projects," "Personal Documents") to keep things tidy.
  • Be consistent with your naming conventions.

Printing a Document

  • Make sure your printer is connected to your computer and turned on.
  • Open the document you want to print.
  • Click on "File" in the menu.
  • Select "Print."
  • A print dialog box will appear where you can choose your printer, the number of copies, and other settings.
  • Click "Print."
