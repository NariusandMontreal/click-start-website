What is a Browser & How to Use It

What Browsers Are

A web browser is a software application that allows you to access and view websites on the internet. Think of it as a window to the online world. Some popular web browsers include:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari (usually comes with Apple devices)
  • Microsoft Edge (the newer version of Internet Explorer)

How to Open a Browser and Use the Address/Search Bar

  • Opening a browser: Look for the browser's icon on your desktop or in your computer's start menu (Windows) or dock (Mac). Double-click the icon to open it.
  • Address Bar: This is usually at the top of the browser window. You can type a website address (URL) directly into it (e.g., www.google.com) and press Enter to go to that website.
  • Search Bar: Sometimes, the address bar also functions as a search bar. If you type keywords instead of a website address (e.g., "weather in Winston-Salem"), the browser will usually use a search engine (like Google) to show you relevant results.

Bookmarking a Page

  • Bookmarking (or saving as a favorite) allows you to quickly access websites you visit often.
  • Look for a star icon ( - you'll need to find an image for this) usually located to the right of the address bar.
  • Click the star icon to bookmark the current page. You might be able to name the bookmark and choose where to save it.
  • To access your bookmarks, look for a "Bookmarks" menu or a folder icon, often located in the browser's toolbar or menu.

Understanding Tabs and How to Switch Between Them

  • Tabs allow you to have multiple websites open in the same browser window. They appear as small rectangles at the top of the browser.
  • To open a new tab, look for a plus (+) icon next to the last open tab or use a keyboard shortcut (Ctrl+T on Windows, Cmd+T on Mac).
  • To switch to a different website, simply click on its tab.

Clearing Browsing History

  • Your browser keeps a record of the websites you've visited (your browsing history).
  • To clear it, look for a "History" option in the browser's menu (usually found by clicking three dots or lines in the top right corner).
  • Select "Clear browsing data" or a similar option.
  • You'll usually be able to choose the time range (e.g., last hour, all time) and what data to clear (e.g., browsing history, cookies, cached images and files).
  • Be careful when clearing cookies, as this might log you out of websites.
