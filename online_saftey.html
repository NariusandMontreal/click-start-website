Online Safety Basics

Recognizing Scam Emails or Fake Websites

  • Be wary of emails asking for personal information (passwords, bank details).
  • Check the sender's email address carefully for unusual spellings or domains.
  • Don't click on links in suspicious emails. Hover your mouse over the link to see where it really goes.
  • Fake websites often have URLs that are slightly different from the real ones.

Creating Strong Passwords

  • Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Make your passwords at least 12 characters long.
  • Avoid using personal information like your name, birthday, or pet's name.
  • Don't use the same password for multiple accounts.

Understanding Antivirus Software (Basics)

  • Antivirus software helps protect your computer from harmful programs (malware).
  • Keep your antivirus software up to date.
  • Run regular scans of your computer.

The Importance of Logging Out on Public Computers

  • When using a public computer (e.g., at a library or school), always log out of your accounts.
  • This prevents others from accessing your personal information after you're done.
  • Close the browser window after logging out for extra security.
